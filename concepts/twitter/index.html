Twitter

Twitter was one of the pioneering social networks to allow people to "connect" their accounts to other websites. On April 15, 2009, Log in with Twitter was introduced. Also referred to as Sign in with Twitter, the name by which it was originally known, this allowed Twitter to be used for social login, and was built on OAuth 1.0, which was already being used for authorizing access to the Twitter API.

Today, use of Twitter for signing in trails substantially behind larger platforms such as Google, Facebook, and Apple. That being said, Twitter remains a widely used option on many websites. Additionally, many websites integrate with Twitter APIs and need users to authorize access, even if they sign in with another authentication method.

Integration with Twitter is done primarily via the Twitter API, which allows developers to access the conversation happening on Twitter. Other APIs are also offered as part of Twitter's platform, such as the Twitter Ads API for programmatically managing advertising campaigns.

Passport can be used to let people sign in with Twitter or obtain authorized access to Twitter APIs on behalf of users, and supports both OAuth 2.0 and OAuth 1.0.
